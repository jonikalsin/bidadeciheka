Could Michael Jordan's brother dunk?

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, May 27, 2024

Michael Jordan, widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball players of all time, is known for his incredible leaping ability and iconic slam dunks.

His older brother, Larry Jordan, also had a passion for basketball, but did he possess the same dunking prowess as his famous sibling? 

Larry Jordan's Basketball Career

Larry Jordan, born in 1957, grew up alongside his younger brother Michael in Wilmington, North Carolina. While Michael pursued a legendary career in the NBA, Larry also had a strong passion for basketball.

He played college basketball at the University of North Carolina at Wilmington, where he demonstrated his skills on the court. Though not as famous as his brother, Larry's basketball abilities were noteworthy in their own right.

Dunking Ability

While it is widely known that Michael Jordan possessed an extraordinary dunking ability, there is limited information available about Larry's dunking prowess.

Given that Larry was not a professional basketball player like his brother, there is not as much public footage or documentation of his playing career. Yet, anecdotal evidence suggests that Larry was indeed capable of dunking the basketball.

Genetics and Athleticism

The Jordan family's athletic genes are well-documented, with both Michael and Larry showcasing their basketball talents. It is widely believed that genetics play a significant role in an individual's athletic abilities, including their jumping and dunking capabilities.

Given their shared genetic makeup, it is reasonable to assume that Larry possessed a degree of the same athleticism that made Michael famous for his high-flying dunks.

Personal Accounts

While there may not be extensive video evidence of Larry Jordan's dunking prowess, there are personal accounts that provide insight into his abilities on the basketball court.

Friends, teammates, and individuals who witnessed Larry play have occasionally shared stories of his skills, including his capability to dunk the basketball. These accounts, while not as widely publicized as Michael's feats, contribute to the understanding of Larry's basketball abilities.

 

While there may not be as much documented evidence of Larry Jordan's dunking ability compared to his younger brother Michael, it is reasonable to believe that he possessed the athleticism and skills to perform dunks on the basketball court.

Given the Jordan family's athletic background, Larry's passion for basketball, and the personal accounts of his playing days, it is plausible that he could indeed dunk. 

In the absence of extensive video documentation or widespread recognition, Larry's basketball career may not have garnered the same level of attention as his brother's, but his contributions to the sport and his own athletic talents should not be overlooked.
