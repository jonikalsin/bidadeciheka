Doug Kingsmore: A Clemson Baseball Legend

Passing of Doug Kingsmore

On December 4, 2023, Doug Kingsmore passed away at the age of 76 after a long illness. Surrounded by his loved ones at his home in Greenville, South Carolina, he peacefully left this world. Kingsmore had been battling a rare form of cancer for several years and had undergone various treatments and surgeries. In his final months, he received hospice care. His family and the Clemson University announced his passing, leaving behind a void that will be deeply felt by all who knew him.

Above all, he cherished his family and his faith, leaving behind a legacy of love, kindness, and generosity.

Doug Kingsmore’s Life and Accomplishments

Doug Kingsmore’s life was marked by remarkable achievements both on and off the baseball field. From his early years to his successful career as a player and entrepreneur, Kingsmore left an indelible mark on the Clemson community and beyond.

Early Life and Family

Born on June 12, 1947, in Greenville, South Carolina, Doug Kingsmore grew up in a family that valued sports and education. His father, Earl Kingsmore, was a former Clemson football player and high school coach, while his mother, Ruth Kingsmore, was a teacher and homemaker. With a strong foundation of support and encouragement, Kingsmore developed a passion for baseball from a young age.

He was not alone in his love for the sport. Kingsmore’s two brothers, Steve and Mike, also played baseball at Clemson, creating a family legacy within the program. Their shared experiences and camaraderie on the field fostered a deep bond that would shape Kingsmore’s future.

Baseball Career and Entrepreneurship

Doug Kingsmore’s baseball career at Clemson from 1965 to 1967 under coach Bill Wilhelm was nothing short of exceptional. He showcased his versatility by playing multiple positions, including pitcher, outfielder, and first baseman. Kingsmore’s talent and dedication earned him recognition as an All-ACC player, leaving a lasting impact on the Clemson Tigers baseball program.

Off the field, Kingsmore’s entrepreneurial spirit led him to build a successful career in the textile industry. His business acumen and determination propelled him to great heights, amassing a fortune and establishing himself as a respected figure in the business world. Despite his achievements, Kingsmore remained grounded and humble, always valuing the importance of family and faith.

Throughout his life, Doug Kingsmore exemplified the values of hard work, dedication, and generosity. His contributions to the Clemson community, both as a player and a philanthropist, will forever be remembered. Kingsmore’s legacy serves as an inspiration to all, reminding us of the power of passion, perseverance, and the impact one individual can have on the lives of many.
